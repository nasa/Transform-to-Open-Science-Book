TOPS Reading List#

Some recommended open science reading spanning from official policy to publications to educational materials. If you are looking for resources to learn how to code, please check out this list.

Open Science Policy and Government Recommendations#

  • UNESCO 2021 Recommendations on Open Science

    • Update: The recording and a summary report of the second meeting of the UNESCO Recommendation on Open Science held on 26 September 2022

    • You may find the recordings and reports from all the Working Group meetings on UNESCO’s Open Science webpage

  • NASEM 2018 Open Science by Design

  • NASEM 2018 Best Practices for a Future Open Code Policy for NASA Space Science

  • NASEM 2021 Developing a Toolkit for Fostering Open Science Practices: Proceedings of a Workshop

  • NASEM 2022 Open Scholarship Priorities and Next Steps: Proceedings of a Workshop–in Brief

  • AGU Article “From open data to open science” 2021 article

  • NASA SMD 2021 Scientific Information policy for the Science Mission Directorate

  • CERN Open Science Policy and accompanying resources

Open Science Training and Handbooks#

  • The Turing Way handbook to reproducible, ethical and collaborative data science

  • A Guide to Using GitHub for Developing and Versioning Data Standards and Reporting Formats

  • Center for Open Science provides a single location for researchers to share their data, code, resources, and pre-prints as well as a variety of resources to enable that

  • Open coding and data learning materials can be found on The Carpentries’ website

  • Online resource list

  • OpenScienceMOOC

  • Foster’s Open Science Toolkit and resources for training others in open science

  • TUDelft’s Open Science Training

  • Eurodoc Ambassador Training

  • UCSB’s Open Science: Best Practices, Data Sovereignty and Co-production book

  • Community Best Practices guide from ESDSWG

  • A video on the Reproducibility and Open Science in Astronomy from ESO ROSA2022

  • A course devoted to equitable data science from UCSB

  • An introduction to open science tutorial by Helena Miton

  • EarthCube has strong resources for learning how to make data more Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reproducible here*

  • The Environmental Data Science Book is, in their words, a “a living, open and community-driven online resource to showcase and support the publication of data, research and open-source tools for collaborative, reproducible and transparent Environmental Data Science.” Although focused on Environmental Data, a lot of the resources are applicable across several fields

  • AGU has collected several open science checklists here

Open Science Communities, Blogs, and Mentorship#

  • Resources for building your own open science community are available from the Center for Scientific Collaboration and Community Engagement

  • Openscapes open science mentorship program

  • The Research Software Alliance has monthly meetings focused on advancing tools for and usage of open-source software for research, more information is here

  • Open Update is Liberate Science’s weekly digest on what’s been going on in the open science space! Listen here

  • Pangeo - an open science community with a weekly community meeting that starts with a 15 min science talk. Meeting schedule is at the bottom of this page.

    • Related, is the Pangeo-Forge data sharing roadmap: https://github.com/pangeo-forge/roadmap.

  • Openscapes - an open source community offering mentorship, teaching, coaching and community organizing centered around open data science

  • Hacktoberfest - a great fun way to first get involved in open source software at any level.

  • The Declaration on Research Assessment (DORA) which recognizes the need to improve the ways in which researchers and the outputs of scholarly research are evaluated

  • Help crowd-source preprint reviews by joining PREreview

  • The Carpentries is a community of instructors who teach foundational skills in coding and data science skills to researchers worldwide, take one of their trainings, join an event, or volunteer to teach in your own community

  • AstroBetter - Tips and tricks for astronomers, including how to use open tools and open-source software

  • The Carpentries Blog - Stay apprised of new trainings and new thinking about old models of teaching

  • The Turing Podcast - Hosted by the Turing Institute, this podcast covers all things computing, including its importance to open science

  • Go FAIR USA has a great collection of news and articles about making research Findable, Accessible, Interoperable and Reproducible here
